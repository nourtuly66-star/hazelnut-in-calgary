Why Calgarians should choose hazelnut

Simple, tasty, and year-round benefits

Calgarians should add hazelnuts to their diet because they’re a simple, tasty way to support health and energy all year round. Packed with vitamin E, hazelnuts help protect skin from Calgary’s cold, dry winters while boosting the immune system during flu season. Their monounsaturated fats, fiber, and magnesium make them heart-healthy, supporting good cholesterol and cardiovascular function. Plus, with plant-based protein and slow-digesting carbs, hazelnuts provide steady energy—perfect for busy workdays or outdoor activities like hiking, biking, and skiing in and around the city. A handful of hazelnuts or a splash of hazelnut flavor in coffee is an easy way for Calgarians to snack smart and stay.

Want to make a hazelnut cake at home?

Easy Hazelnut Cake (Base Recipe)

Ingredients

  • 1 cup hazelnut meal (or finely ground toasted hazelnuts)
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 2 tsp baking powder
  • ¼ tsp salt
  • 3 large eggs
  • ½ cup milk
  • ½ cup neutral oil or melted butter
  • 1 tsp vanilla
  • Topping: ½–¾ cup chocolate-hazelnut spread, ½ cup sliced or chopped hazelnuts

Method

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a rectangular pan with parchment.
  2. Mix dry ingredients (flour, hazelnut meal, sugar, baking powder, salt) in one bowl.
  3. Whisk wet ingredients (eggs, milk, oil/butter, vanilla) in another bowl.
  4. Combine just until smooth, pour into pan, and bake 45–55 minutes (loaf size) until a toothpick comes out mostly clean.
  5. While warm, spread with chocolate-hazelnut spread and sprinkle with nuts. Cool and dust with icing sugar if desired.

All ingredients can be easily found in supermarkets around Calgary.
